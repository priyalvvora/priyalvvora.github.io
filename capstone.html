The Brand

An Indie dyer with a devoted community

Ruby and Roses is the studio of Addy — an Indie dyer who specializes in finely speckled colorways and mini-skein sets, inspired by travel destinations and pop culture themes. The brand sells through Shopify with a small but emotionally engaged community of knitters and crocheters who follow new drops, color clubs, and seasonal collections.

The challenge: the brand's word-of-mouth growth had outpaced its analytical infrastructure. They needed a clearer read on which customers, products, and channels were actually driving value — and where the next chapter of growth should come from.

Visit rubyandrosesyarn.com →
Project Scope

What we set out to answer

  • Competitive position.Where does Ruby and Roses stand against Sewrella, Tanis Fiber Art, Explorer Knits, and other Indie dyers?
  • Channel ROI.Which digital channels (Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Mailchimp, SEO) actually move revenue — and which look busy without contributing?
  • Customer value.Who are the highest-LTV customers, and which segments deserve different lifecycle treatment?
  • Cross-sell mechanics.Which products co-purchase most strongly, and where are the natural bundles?
  • Order fulfillment.Can we predict which orders are at risk of unfulfillment so the team can intervene proactively?
  • Incremental impact.What revenue lift can the brand expect from disciplined digital promotion?
01 — RFM Segmentation

Eight customer segments, eight playbooks

An RFM model on Shopify transaction history (Aug 2021 – Feb 2022) revealed clear groupings by Recency, Frequency, and Monetary Value. 53% of the base sits in the "Average" group — the leverage is in moving them up the value ladder, while protecting the small but high-yield Top and High Monetary Value segments.

Customer Distribution

Segment-level activation playbook

Each segment received a tailored marketing recommendation aligned to its RFM profile, with strategies ranging from VIP membership for top tier to win-back discounts for at-risk loyalists.

Top + Good Customers

Build a membership tier

96 customers driving disproportionate value. A paid loyalty program with early access to drops protects retention and creates predictable revenue.

High Monetary Value

Cross-sell premium bundles

109 customers with high AOV but only 1–2 purchases. Curated bundles and "complete the collection" prompts can lift frequency without discounting.

Average Customers

Lifecycle nurture & upgrade

869 customers, the biggest pool. Email + retargeting that surfaces seasonal collections and bundles moves them toward "frequent" status.

Recent Customers

Second-purchase trigger

330 first-time buyers — high churn risk. A discounted recommendation 30 days post-purchase based on category affinity converts trial into habit.

At-Risk Top Customers

Win-back with personalization

165 historical high-value buyers who've gone quiet. Reach out with new collection previews and a discount code referencing past favorites.

Frequent Buyers

Bundle pricing to lift AOV

35 customers who buy often but at lower AOV. Volume discounts and product set pricing convert frequency into monetary value.

03 — Predictive Modeling

Predicting at-risk orders before they slip

Of 12,104 Shopify orders, 99 went unfulfilled — under 1%, but each one is a CX moment. A logistic regression trained on financial, shipping, and source features flags the at-risk minority with very high precision, enabling proactive inventory and customer-service intervention.

Operational Use

Drivers Identified

What signals risk

Shipping country, shipping method, payment method, and risk level were the strongest predictors. International orders and certain payment paths needed closer attention.

Business Impact

Protecting CX

Catching even half the unfulfilled orders before customers notice means ~50 saved CX incidents per cycle, plus protected lifetime value from a sensitive Indie audience.

04 — Sales Trend

The shape of demand — strongly seasonal

Across the consulting window, monthly order volume climbed steadily from August into a sharp December peak (389 orders, $31.9K revenue) before falling off as the team's holiday-driven collection cycle closed. The seasonality validates concentrating paid spend and email cadence around the Oct–Dec lift, with a Q1 win-back motion to recapture momentum.

07 — Strategic Recommendations

Where Ruby and Roses should focus next

Synthesizing customer, product, and channel findings, six priorities emerged for the next two quarters.

01

Launch a tiered loyalty program

Membership for Top and Good segments with early access to drops and exclusive Indie collaborations.

02

Productize the top bundles

Pre-bundled SKUs for the highest-confidence pairings — surfacing on PDP and via Mailchimp.

03

Build the second-purchase journey

30-day automated email with category-affinity recommendations to convert Recent into Frequent.

04

Reallocate paid social to Instagram

Cut Facebook spend, run Instagram Ads targeting Sewrella / Tanis Fiber Art lookalikes.

05

Schedule for peak intent windows

Concentrate ads and email sends at 3pm local time, Sunday and Tuesday — observed activity peaks.

06

Proactive fulfillment intervention

Use the linear regression to flag at-risk orders pre-fulfillment, triggering inventory and CX outreach.
